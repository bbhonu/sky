  • 7

    Partial Lunar Eclipse

    This lunar eclipse is a result of Earth passing between the Sun and Moon. This position causes Earth to obscure the light of the Sun and cast a shadow on the Moon’s surface. The moon may appear to have a bite or portion taken out of it.

    The eclipse will be most visible in eastern Africa, central Asia, and Australia. It will not be visible in the western hemisphere.

  • 21

    The Great American Eclipse

    This total solar eclipse which has been dubbed “The Great American Eclipse,” is a rare event which occurs when the moon completely blocks the sun. The moon will appear as a black circle, with the sun’s corona radiating at its perimeter. A total solar eclipse this visible hasn’t occurred in the United States since 1979.

    At least a partial eclipse will be visible in North America and parts of northern South America. From any location, the total eclipse will last for a maximum of two minutes.

    Optimal visibility is dependent on location. For more information visit eclipse2017.nasa.gov.
